About Me

Software Developer and Electrical Engineer

Matthew Staniszewski

Project Lead

I am originally from Wisconsin and moved to California in 2014. My family and I moved here for better schools for our daughters, better opportunities and the better weather.
Volunteering and helping others is important to me. I am an extremely active member in the Eastshore-PTA for three years, Girl Scouts for 10 years and help students at LearningFuze.
I have been in the engineering field with my associates in electrical engineering since 2006. Every year since then there has been projects or problems with computers or equipment that fueled my desire to learn programing. To build and program electronics became my biggest passion.

Completing the LearningFuze boot camp program has allow me to pursue my passion as a software developer. This was also one of the most challenging and satisfying accomplishments I have completed. Every day was a new challenge and something new to learn and not just in programming but in team building skills, communication and other tools to use to become an effective programmer. All the work and hours I put in prepared me for completing a final project I designed for the final weeks at LearningFuze. Out of 16 projects submitted, mine was 1 of 4 that maid the cut as a final team projects. As the team lead I over saw and managed the project with feedback and opinions of the team while keeping constant communication on progress and updates. Together we were able to accomplished in 3 weeks our MVP with enough time to and add in login verification before our last week to cut added features!

What I learned through LearningFuze was all applied to create my main project. As a team reviewed and updated feature sets using the Agile methodology. First week was frame work design for front and back end along with setting up the MVP feature sets into tasks. Second week was completing the MVP sprints. Final week hard cut any adding of features and finished styling, debugging and error handling. Front and back end wire frames were created using Figma and DBDesigner to create the UI, UE and data base. Sprints were created from an MVP feature set list to set priority, time tracking, documentation and work performed with MeisterTask. My main focus was backend with PHP with session, SQL and myPHPAdmin for the final project. It was a hard choice between PHP or Node.js but choose PHP. My goal is to become really good at PHP and then start improving my Node.js skills. I helped my two team members on on the front end when I could. For the front end we chose to use React with Redux, React-forms, Axios and React-Links. The project was a success and appreciate having a team with as much focus, communication and dedication as I have.

With all that I have learned I now have a fun project I am working on. I can now improve my programing skills by building and programing electronics. I am creating a CAD drawing to 3D print a car shaped container to mount a Raspberry Pi and an Andromeda. There is a motion sensors, distance sensor, a camera, stepper motors, temperature probes, laser sensors, relays and more. The goal, to remotely control and communicate with the devices and sensors with a UI. Sensors will be integrated and programed to aid and control the functionality of the motors and camera. I am excited to see how much I can do with this project.

WIP - Board Game Scanner - Hack-A-Thon Project

The board game scanner allows you to scan the bar code on the box of a game. After scanning, you will see the title, an image of the game along with the minimum and maximum price it sold for. You then can look for locations to purchase that specific game. Finally you can have instant access to YouTube videos of reviews and game play to learn how to play the game.

The front end frame work with HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript-OOP and Ajax for GET and POST requests. Used PHP to communicate with the UPCitemdb API. Used quaggaJS API for scanning the game board bar codes. YouTube API to pull videos. Google Maps API to find locations to purchase the game.
