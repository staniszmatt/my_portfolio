About Me

Software Developer and Electrical Engineer

Matthew Staniszewski

Project Lead

I am originally from Wisconsin and moved to California in 2014. My family and I moved here for better schools for our daughters, better opportunities and the better weather.
Volunteering and helping others is important to me. I am an extremely active member in the Eastshore-PTA for three years, Girl Scouts for 10 years and help students at LearningFuze.
I have been in the engineering field with my associates in electrical engineering since 2006. Every year since then there has been projects or problems with computers or equipment that fueled my desire to learn programing. To build and program electronics became my biggest passion.

Completing the LearningFuze boot camp program has allow me to pursue my passion as a software developer. This was also one of the most challenging and satisfying accomplishments I have completed. Every day was a new challenge and something new to learn and not just in programming but in team building skills, communication and other tools to use to become an effective programmer. All the work and hours I put in prepared me for completing a final project I designed for the final weeks at LearningFuze. Out of 16 projects submitted, mine was 1 of 4 that maid the cut as a final team projects. As the team lead I over saw and managed the project with feedback and opinions of the team while keeping constant communication on progress and updates. There is a fun project I am working on to improve my programing skills by building and programing electronics. I am creating a CAD drawing to 3D print a car shaped container to mount a Raspberry Pi and an Andromeda. There is a motion sensors, distance sensor, a camera, stepper motors, temperature probes, laser sensors, relays and more. The goal, to remotely control and communicate with the devices and sensors with a UI. Sensors will be integrated and programed to aid and control the functionality of the motors and camera.
